Death Note

Hello! This is my first shrine, so I wanted it to be special. The two great loves of my life are Death Note and Poptropica, so it was a no-brainer I start with one of those.

Death Note was my first anime, discovered before I even know what anime was, watched in 240p 3-part-per-episode YouTube uploads. I was a morbid little child who loved horror, so you just know the second 8 year old Vesperings head about "a notebook that kills people" she was sold on it. I tried to very excitedly tell people all about it at school the next day, but they just didn't seem to be as into it as I was.

Ever since then, it's just been a part of me. In high school, I was just associated with it in my weeb (affectionate) circles. It was my show. I mean it when I say I wouldn't be who I am without it, Death Note is even the reason I got into Visual Kei and then more broadly goth and metal -- who would I be without my little tunes? Obsessed with The World, I decided to check out the rest of Nightmare's discography, which led me to The GazettE and exist†trace, who I'd spend hours admiring on Pinterest. Now, my V系 playlist is near 600 songs and counting, and I owe it all to the silly little killing people diary show.

Wow, you speak so highly of it! Is Death Note really that good?

Um..... hmm... I uh... have to go return some video tapes. Seriously though, I maintain the best way to enjoy Death Note is to think of it as a comedy. Which isn't difficult because it is so fucking funny. Who cares about morality and rooting for a side when you can giggle at Ryuk's one liners and the long, ridiculous monologues? Okay wait I'm not advocating for media illiteracy here, I'm just saying it's a fun show and you should have fun watching it. And yes there are a lot of great ~themes~ and it is very entertaining because it is a solid piece of source material, but in my opinion half its greatness comes from the fandom. The L/Light internal monologue format NEVER gets old and without it, the anime would not be as well loved. The anime takes itself seriously as a whole, but is at its best when it doesn't.

What about the characters? You're being suspiciously quiet about them.

Calm down, I'm getting there. From the moment I first saw him, I ended up absorbing L into my personality. We had the same birthday, were both the smartest people we knew (listen, I'm not a narcissist, it's just not that difficult when you're in Year 4, okay?), sugar addicts, and even look alike. No kidding, I put on a white T-shirt and look like I'm cosplaying L. Or Jeff the Killer.... As a kid I found Misa annoying and wished Naomi lasted longer (I still miss her come back babygirl), but now I'm old enough to realise Misa is just the victim of bad writing. She is so compelling when she's first introduced but gets bimbofied 2 seconds later. So much wasted potential. She is childish and manipulative and so intelligent and it's all wasted to make her a whiny loser when she could be so much more. Also she managed to get two shinigami to fall for her? And she just doesn't care? Misa Amane I wish I was your writer I could make you so good.
